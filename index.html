Dental Technology

Context

Technology in general dentistry is in both terms of Software and Hardware, each bringing an extremely helpful impact on the field as a whole.

Current innovations

Software:

Description: Software in dentistry is essential. It makes the lives of dental professionals much easier, before the development of this software, things like procedures, diagnosis, and billing would be less accurate and less efficient.

  1. OpenDental: Uses a self-service portal to provide specific patients dental charts. This also allows billing departments to easily access what procedure a patient has done so that there would be a proper bill to the insurance.
  2. Colgate Magik: Uses augmentative- follow along videos for kids to actively brush their teeth in an efficient and healthy way.
  3. Logicon Caries Detector: Uses sensors to identify the location of cavities in x-rays.

Hardware:

Description: Hardware is the most if not one of the most important things in a dental atmosphere. These tools are what clearly get the procedure done in a safe and fast manner. With the interest of both the patients comfort, recovery time, and overall healing after the dental visit.

  1. X-ray: Takes photos of different teeth to allow dentists to check for cavities and abnormalities.
  2. Laser: More accurate, less painful, and less healing times.
  3. Dental crown: Acrylic top for a tooth that surrounds the entirety of it.

My Idea

Future Technology:

Robotics Dental x-ray

  1. Takes perfectly accurate x-rays.
  2. Uses robotic arms that spin around the chair and the patient while they are sitting in the chair.
  3. The patient's head would need to be sitting in some sort of “holder”.
  4. In traditional x-ray use an assistant must manually move a sensor to different angles in the patient's mouth, to combat this the patient will bite down on one mouth guard shaped sensor so that it doesn't have to be moved. This mouth piece would also need to be disposable. Making the procedure must faster and efficient.
  5. There would be a small screen across from the patient holder to explain step by step what the robotic arm is doing, so that the patient doesn't get too alarmed when there's something happening to them. This would also ensure the patients comfort.
